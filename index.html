05About these metrics
Static · what & why, not data

Why these four KPIs

A campaign dashboard exists to answer three questions in under a minute: is the funnel healthy this week, which campaign is winning, and where is value leaking? The four headline metrics map directly to those questions. LP Clicks measures whether ads are working; Installs measures whether the landing page delivers on the ad; Activations measures whether the product delivers on the landing page; and End-to-End CVR is the shared accountability metric for the whole team. We separate the three funnel stages because each has a different owner and a different remedy — a click-to-install drop points at marketing, an install-to-activation drop points at product. Conflating them obscures who needs to act.

How they measure campaign success

Together these metrics span the full user journey from ad impression to product value, which is what "campaign success" actually means at scale. Volume metrics (clicks, installs, activations) tell us whether the campaign is reaching enough users; conversion rates (click→install, install→activate, click→activation) tell us how efficiently it converts attention into adoption. The diagnostics below — anomaly cohorts, day-of-week patterns, and the retention curve — exist to localize where any leak is happening so the right team can act on it. Filtering by campaign or window lets the same framework answer the same three questions for any slice of the data.
